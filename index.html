Jalāl ad-Dīn Muhammad Rūmī

Jalāl ad-Dīn Muhammad Rūmī (Rumi) devoted his life to Islam through both knowledge and spirituality. He was a respected scholar who taught the Qur’an and Islamic law, but he also followed the path of Sufism, focusing on love and closeness to Allah. Through his famous works like the Masnavi, he transformed Islamic teachings into beautiful poetry that speaks about faith, unity, and the human soul. His life shows that true devotion to Islam is not only in learning, but also in feeling and living its message.

M.J. Rumi Life

Jalāl ad-Dīn Muhammad Rūmī was born in 1207 in Balkh (now in Afghanistan) and was raised in a deeply religious family. His father was an Islamic scholar, and Rumi followed the same path, becoming a respected teacher of the Qur’an, Hadith, and Islamic law. He later moved to Konya (in present-day Turkey), where he taught students and guided people in religious matters. A major turning point in his life came when he met Shams of Tabriz, who inspired him to explore the deeper spiritual side of Islam through Sufism. After this, Rumi began expressing his devotion through poetry, focusing on love for Allah, inner peace, and unity. He wrote many famous works, especially the Masnavi, which teaches Islamic values through stories and spiritual lessons. He passed away in 1273 in Konya, but his teachings and poetry continue to inspire people around the world even today.

M.J. Rumi Contribution in Islamic Literature

Masnavi

This is Rumi’s most famous and important work. It is a six-volume poem containing thousands of verses. It includes stories, parables, and teachings based on Islamic ideas and explains lessons about faith, morality, and love of Allah.

Divan-e Shams-e Tabrizi

A large collection of emotional and lyrical poems dedicated to Shams of Tabriz. It expresses deep feelings of love, longing, and connection with God.

Fihi Ma Fihi

A collection of Rumi’s talks and teachings that explains Islamic ideas in a simple and direct way. It is less poetic and more like guidance and advice.
